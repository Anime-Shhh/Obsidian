To find a unit vector in the direction of _any_ given vector, you divide the original vector by its own magnitude (length). Think of it like this: if a vector has a length of 5, you divide it by 5 to shrink its length down to exactly 1, while keeping it pointing in the exact same direction. Here is the formula: $$\text{Unit Vector} = \frac{\text{Vector}}{\text{Magnitude of Vector}}$$ In this problem, we already did the heavy lifting in parts (a) and (b):

1. **The Vector:** From part (a), our vector is $\langle 2, 18, -8 \rangle$.
2. **The Magnitude:** From part (b), the length of that vector is $\sqrt{392}$.
