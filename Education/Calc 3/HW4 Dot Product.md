### **Problem 1**

**Given:** $\vec{u} = \langle 0, -9, -10 \rangle$ $\vec{v} = \langle 4, -11, -12 \rangle$ **To find $\vec{u} \cdot \vec{v}$:** The dot product is found by multiplying the corresponding components (x with x, y with y, z with z) and adding them together: $$ \vec{u} \cdot \vec{v} = (u_x \cdot v_x) + (u_y \cdot v_y) + (u_z \cdot v_z) $$ Substitute the values: $$ \vec{u} \cdot \vec{v} = (0)(4) + (-9)(-11) + (-10)(-12) $$ $$ \vec{u} \cdot \vec{v} = 0 + 99 + 120 $$ $$ \vec{u} \cdot \vec{v} = 219 $$ **Answer:** $\vec{u} \cdot \vec{v} =$ **$219$** ---

### **Problem 2**

**Given:** $\vec{a} = -6\vec{i} - 2\vec{j} + 8\vec{k}$ $\vec{b} = 6\vec{i} + 5\vec{j} + 1\vec{k}$ **To find $\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b}$:** Even though this uses standard basis vector notation ($\vec{i}, \vec{j}, \vec{k}$), the process is exactly the same as in component form. You just multiply the matching coefficients and add them up. $$ \vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = (a_i \cdot b_i) + (a_j \cdot b_j) + (a_k \cdot b_k) $$ Substitute the values: $$ \vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = (-6)(6) + (-2)(5) + (8)(1) $$ $$ \vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = -36 - 10 + 8 $$ $$ \vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = -46 + 8 $$ $$ \vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = -38 $$ **Answer:** $\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} =$ **$-38$**
