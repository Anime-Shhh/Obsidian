## 4 major data types
### **1. Nominal (Categorical)**
- **Definition:** Labels or names only. No math, no ranking.
- **Key Property:** Identity.
- **Examples:** Eye color, Zip code, Gender, Genre.
- **Viz:** Bar Chart, Pie Chart.
### **2. Ordinal**
- **Definition:** Data with a specific order/rank, but the distance between points is unknown.
- **Key Property:** Relative Rank.
- **Examples:** Satisfaction (Sad → Happy), Spice level, Finish position (1st, 2nd).
- **Viz:** Sorted Bar Chart.
### **3. Interval**
- **Definition:** Numeric data with known, equal distances between values, but **no true zero**.
- **Key Property:** Addition/Subtraction (not multiplication).
- **Examples:** Temp (∘F, ∘C), IQ Score, Dates.
- **Viz:** Line Graph, Histogram.
### **4. Ratio**
- **Definition:** Numeric data with equal distances **and** a **true zero** (0 = nothing).
- **Key Property:** Multiplication/Division (e.g., "twice as much").
- **Examples:** Height, Weight, Salary, Age, Distance.
- **Viz:** Scatter Plot, Heat Map.
